回答：The question refers to Plato’s dialogue *Euthyphro*, in which Socrates and Euthyphro discuss the nature of piety (the holy, or τὸ ὅσιον — *to hosion*).

In the dialogue, Euthyphro proposes several definitions of the holy. One key moment occurs when Socrates asks whether the holy is a part of the just (τὸ δίκαιον — *to dikaion*), and Euthyphro agrees.

Specifically, at **Euthyphro 12a–d**, Socrates leads Euthyphro to accept that:

- The holy is *a part* of the just (i.e., all holy things are just, but not all just things are holy).
- Justice itself is a broader concept, and holiness/purity/piety is a specific subset — namely, that part of justice concerned with attending to the gods (e.g., prayer, sacrifice, proper reverence).

Socrates says (in essence):  
> “Then the holy is a part of the just?”  
> Euthyphro replies: “Yes.”  
> Socrates: “So if we were asked, ‘What part of the just is the holy?’, we could say: that part concerned with care of the gods…”
