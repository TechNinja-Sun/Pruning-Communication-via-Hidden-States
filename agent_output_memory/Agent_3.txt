回答：The question refers to Plato’s dialogue *Euthyphro*, in which Socrates and Euthyphro discuss the nature of piety (the holy or the pious). A key moment occurs when Socrates asks Euthyphro to define “the holy” (τὸ ὅσιον), and through dialectic, he leads Euthyphro to propose that the holy is a part of justice (or the just).

Specifically, at *Euthyphro* 12a–d, Socrates gets Euthyphro to agree:

> “Then the holy is a part of the just?”  
> “It seems so.”  
> “Then if the holy is a part of the just, we can say what part of the just it is…”  

Socrates then suggests that the holy (or pious) is that part of justice concerned with attending to the gods — as opposed to the rest of justice, which concerns attending to human affairs.

Thus, Socrates explicitly identifies the holy as **a part of what is just**.

✅ Correct answer: **B. what is just.**

Other options:
- A. prudent (φρόνησις) — related to practical wisdom, not directly identified as the genus of the holy.
- C. beautiful (καλόν) — while piety may be beautiful, Plato does not define it as a part of the beautiful in this dialogue.
- D. legal — anachronistic and not used in this conceptual taxonomy; Athenian law is conventional, whereas Socrates seeks a universal, essential definition.

**Answer: B. what is just.**
