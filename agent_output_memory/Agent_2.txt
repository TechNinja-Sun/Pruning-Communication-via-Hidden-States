回答：The question refers to Plato’s dialogue *Euthyphro*, in which Socrates and Euthyphro discuss the nature of piety (the holy or the pious). In their dialectic, Socrates presses Euthyphro to define “the holy” (τὸ ὅσιον), and through a series of proposals and refutations, one key claim emerges:

Socrates suggests — and Euthyphro agrees — that **the holy is a part of the just** (or “what is just”).

Specifically, at *Euthyphro* 12a–d, after rejecting earlier definitions, Euthyphro proposes that “the holy is what is loved by all the gods,” but Socrates challenges that. Later, Euthyphro offers:  
> “What is dear to the gods is holy, and what is not dear to them is unholy.”  
But Socrates pushes for a more essential definition — not based on divine approval, but on its place within moral reality.

Crucially, at *Euthyphro* 11e–12a, Euthyphro says:  
> “I think the holy is a part of the just…”  
and Socrates accepts this as a promising starting point, saying it’s “a fine statement” — and then proceeds to ask *which part* of the just it is (e.g., the part concerned with care of the gods, etc.).

So the correct answer is:

**B. what is just.**
